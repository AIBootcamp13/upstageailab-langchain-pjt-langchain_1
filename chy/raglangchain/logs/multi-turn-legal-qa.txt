The user inquired whether, even in the absence of criminal liability, if a case is lost, the attorney's fees should only be partially borne by the client. According to the **Civil Procedure Act** Articles 109 (Burden of Litigation Costs) and 110 (Determination of Litigation Costs), the losing party is generally responsible for litigation costs. However:  
1. Only the portion of the attorney’s fees falling within the court-established limit can be claimed from the opposing party.  
2. Amounts exceeding this limit, as specified in the client-attorney contract, remain the client’s responsibility.  
3. The exact allocation may vary depending on case specifics, scale, and contractual terms, necessitating a review of relevant documents.  

This clarification focuses on civil litigation, not criminal cases where attorney fees are typically the client’s sole responsibility.
